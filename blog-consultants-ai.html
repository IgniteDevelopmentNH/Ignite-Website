For New Hampshire Consultants

How Consultants Can Leverage AI to Sell More & Work Less

Joshua Berry
Co-Founder · June 22, 2026

I've always been passionate about the field of consulting. Through UNH, family friends, and networking events around Southern New Hampshire, I've gotten to know a lot of consultants doing incredible work. Management consultants, marketing advisors, financial strategists, people running their own firms out of offices in Manchester, Nashua, Portsmouth, the Seacoast.

What really struck me was how much they all care about their client relationships. They're smart, they're relationship-driven, they actually invest in understanding the people they work with. But they're also all saying the same thing: "Yeah, AI is definitely the future. We should probably figure that out."

And then... nothing happens. They don't use it. Or they try some random tool, it doesn't fit how they work, and they move on.

I realized the problem wasn't that these consultants didn't understand AI. It was that nobody was building AI solutions for them. For small NH firms. For people who can't afford a six-month implementation or a six-figure bill. For people who just want to work smarter, not reinvent their entire business.

That's why we built Ignite. I'm so excited about what we're doing because I genuinely believe this can change how consultants work in our community.

Here's what we've found: AI can save you hours every week. Both on the client-facing work that represents your brand, and the internal busywork that slows you down. And it doesn't have to be expensive or complicated if you do it right.

Let me walk you through how this actually works.

The Problem: You're Doing Everything Yourself

If you're running a consulting firm in Southern New Hampshire, you're probably solo or a small team. Which means you're wearing every hat: selling, delivering, managing, following up, writing proposals, making presentations, handling admin.

That's the strength of NH consulting. You can be responsive. You can be personal. You actually know your clients.

It's also killing you. And I say that because I've heard it from so many consultants I respect.

Here's what the math looks like:

On Client Work

  • 6–10 hours per major proposal (writing, formatting, revising, getting it to sound right)
  • 4–8 hours per client report (designing, pulling data, synthesizing findings, polishing)
  • 2–3 hours per presentation (building slides, sourcing graphics, getting the layout right)

That's roughly 40% of your non-billable time spent just making deliverables look and sound right. Not thinking. Not strategizing. Not selling. Formatting.

If you're billing at $150–200/hour, that's costing you $2,500–4,000/month in lost billable time. A $30,000–50,000 annual hit. Just on representation.

On Internal Operations

Then there's the stuff nobody mentions: following up with prospects who haven't replied, tracking which clients need check-ins, organizing research, documenting your processes so you can actually hire someone without losing your mind.

The average consultant loses 4–6 prospects per year just from forgotten follow-ups. That's money you left on the table because you forgot to send an email.

Put it together? You're leaving $50,000–80,000 on the table every year just on inefficiency. That's real money for a small NH firm. And it's strongly frustrating to watch happen when it doesn't have to.

Solution 1: AI Brand Development

For client-facing work + your website

Here's what we do: we build a customized AI agent trained on how you actually work. Your voice, your quality bar, your methodology.

Then we completely revamp your website at the same time.

Now when you need a proposal, you give the AI a few key details. Client name, scope, timeline. It drafts it in your exact style. You spend 30 minutes reviewing and refining. Not 10 hours writing from scratch.

Same with reports. Same with presentations. Same with any client-facing document.

Your website stops being a placeholder and becomes something that actually represents who you are. Something that makes prospects want to work with you. I'm excited about this part especially because your website should be working for you, not just sitting there.

What actually changes

  • You quote clients faster. Faster response means you win more deals. Studies show 75% of buying decisions are influenced by response time.
  • Everything sounds like you. Your website, your proposals, your reports all speak in the same voice. Clients trust you more because you feel real and consistent.
  • Your website actually works for you. Instead of being background noise, your site is actively helping you land clients.
  • You stop staying up late polishing deliverables. You have your evenings back. That alone is worth it.
A realistic example

Say a consultant is spending 10 hours per major proposal. With the AI Brand system, that can drop to 2–3 hours of reviewing and customizing — roughly 7 hours back in the week to spend on strategy and selling.

The Brand package is about taking the busywork out of representing your expertise, so you can focus on actually doing the expert work.

Solution 2: Internal Operations System

For efficiency & scale

This is where things get real for small teams.

When you're solo or a two-person shop, you can't afford to lose prospects to forgotten follow-ups. You can't hire someone without training them on everything in your head. You can't scale without it becoming chaos. I've watched consultants struggle with this and it's preventable.

We build an AI system trained on how your business actually works. Your processes, your knowledge, your way of doing things. This system automates the repetitive internal work and integrates with how you already operate.

Real examples

  • Prospect follow-up: The system tracks which leads haven't responded and drafts personalized follow-up emails in your voice. You're not manually remembering who to follow up with.
  • Client check-ins: Reminds you to reach out to clients on your schedule, with context about what you last discussed.
  • Process documentation: Turns your knowledge into documented playbooks and training materials. When you finally hire someone, they can actually learn from you instead of trial and error.
  • Research organization: Pulls together industry insights, competitor intel, and client context so you're prepared and sharp before every meeting.

What actually changes

  • You stop losing prospects to forgotten follow-ups. Companies with strong follow-up systems close 30–40% more deals.
  • New hires are productive 3x faster. You can actually scale without reinventing yourself every time someone joins.
  • Your team focuses on billable work instead of admin. More revenue per person.
  • Your knowledge doesn't leave when an employee does.

Do You Need Both? (Probably Not to Start)

You don't necessarily need both packages right away.

Pick AI Brand Development if

  • You're drowning in client deliverables (proposals, reports, presentations)
  • You want to be more responsive to prospects
  • Your website feels like it's not really helping you
  • Consistency of how you present yourself matters

Pick Internal Operations if

  • You're losing deals or prospects to follow-up gaps
  • You're planning to hire and need to actually document what you do

Do both if

  • You want to reclaim hours on every front
  • You're scaling and need efficiency everywhere
